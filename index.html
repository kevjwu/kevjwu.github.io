Understanding “Fedspeak”: Identifying the Sources of Market Sentiment in Central Bank Communications

Senior thesis, presented to the Harvard University Department of Applied Mathematics (April 2014)

I examined the effect of one of the Federal Open Market Committee’s post-meeting announcement—one of the most highly anticipated economic release in the financial community—on short-term stock market returns. Whereas the majority of existing research uses Twitter or financial news to determine the polarity of Fed statements, I used stock price returns themselves to categorize statements as either positive or negative. The other novelty of this approach was the use of sparse regression (LASSO) to identify of a small number of influential phrases from central bank announcements. I find that although sparse regression is able to detect the presence of key phrases and sentences from Fed communications, none of the textual features that were selected were found to be statistically significant.
